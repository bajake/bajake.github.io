Octopress - Coding for N00bs

Feb 15th, 2013

When it comes to coding, I am what the interwebs refer to as a newbie, a newcomer, a novice. Sure, I took an HTML class in college and made a few crappy websites but since then, all I have been able to do is go into some content management system (CMS) and change a few lines of HTML or CSS. I have never really understood how it all works. A few months ago, I decided to attempt a change in my ignorance.

Since I use applications, websites, and databases everyday in my job, I know good technology when I see it. Jack Dorsey, the founder of Twitter and Square, says it best,

The best technologies disappear, they fade into the background and are relevant when you use them and get out of the way when you don’t.

That is a great saying but how do you create the application in the first place? I needed somewhere to start.

After much searching I stumbled upon a great site by Zed A. Shaw who promised that I could learn to code the hard way. I was intrigued and jumped right in. That once scary terminal program and I became good friends and like a boy and his hunting dog, we were shooting down python exercises left and right. print this, import that, it was great! Then all of a sudden I stopped. I didn’t really want to go on. For weeks, I couldn’t figure out why I didn’t want to learn to code anymore. Then it hit me, I wasn’t actually creating anything tangible. These exercises were great learning tools but I never felt satisfied with the little bit of basic python knowledge I accumulated. I needed to learn and create at the same time.

Octopress to the rescue! Here is an application that is based off Ruby, uses Jekyll, ties with GitHub and Heroku and even introduces the concepts of Markdown. Also, in order to create new posts, I have to get reacquainted with my friend the terminal. Finally a tangible challenge! It took me a few days to follow the directions but I now have a blog that I can be proud of, all while learning a shit ton about how it all works. I even customized the theme to differentiate myself.

The blog you see before you will be a playground for my continued coding education as well as a place to post my thoughts and ideas on how technology and the physical world intersect, hence the name. So while I am most likely still considered a newbie in the coding world, I look forward to where the rabbit hole will take me next.
